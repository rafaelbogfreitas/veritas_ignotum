Veritas Ignotum GDPR policy

Foreword

The endeavour of Veritas Ignotum ltd (VI) is to facilitate an individually tailored consultancy, whilst ensuring that the highest levels of protection are offered. All effort has been made to protect the individuals sensitive data in accordance with the Data Protection Act 2018 (DPA), Law and General Data Protection Regulations (GDPR); this charter outlines the various procedures and protocols, VI and its consultants implement. This charter is applicable to VI and in its associated media including but not limited to websites and future communication applications (such as messenger, chat applications). This charter stands foremost in relation to specific VI properties, if engaged via referral in which case the forwarding agent policy shall control.

Personal privacy is of the upmost importance to our organisation. We ensure that all aspects of the business are conducted alongside the rules and regulations set out in the DPA and GDPR, this in turn allows VI to both protect your sensitive data and respect in its upkeep.

This charter aims to provide you with a concise & genuine document of how, when and for what reasons we may collect sensitive data of those who seek out our services and/or engage with our website. This charter will also explain key areas such as; how we utilise your data, how we maintain its security and what we may need to disclose your data.

Disclaimer

By the use of our website, www.veritasignotum.co.uk, you are in agreement with the information laid out in this charter, and thus are willingly providing consent in order to ‘Opt in’ (Article 7) for the use of all and any data you choose to provide.

How & why your data is collected.

In order to engage with the services of Veritas Ignotum you may be required to provide sensitive data. This is in order to establish mutual contact (E.G. in order for you to request information of our services or allowing VI to respond to this). At no time are you obliged to provide this information, however VI may request such information in order to provide you with correct, necessary & timely information and in order to respond to any inquires you have submitted.

No sensitive data in any respect, in part or full, will be collected or stored that is supplied by 3rd party sources. All data managed and protected by VI will be provided knowingly and explicitly from the individual.

Use of your data. Article 6

Your information will be appropriated as such in:

  • In response to enquiries
  • To meet the obligations set out in a contract of service agreed to by both parties.
  • To ascertain evaluation or testimonial from services rendered.
  • To notify you of addendums of service pertinent to the individual.

Length of time data is kept

Timelines that data is held vary depending on the nature of exchange. Data provided in a correspondence will only be held until such times as this has come to a natural end (no further information is required to be provided by VI). Basic sensitive information (name, address & contact details) provided in regards to services and/or consultancy must be kept for a minimum of 6 years in accordance with UK tax laws (after this time has elapsed it will be destroyed). We do not hold your information for marketing purposes.

Who has access to your data?

VI would like to reiterate that we ‘Respect & Protect’ your sensitive information. To that end any information you have provided in correspondence to us will not, under any circumstances, be sold rented or divulged to any 3rd parties for their marketing requirement.

In respect to the nature of services VI offers, there may be a requirement to engage with external agencies in order to deliver on our contractual obligations (Article 15(1)(c)). If at such time this is to occur, you will be contacted in order to express explicit consent of this exchange. Furthermore, your data once submitted is exempt from this charter and will be taken under the known secure policy of the receiving parties.

Your access, change and deletion of data

It is your right to request for a copy of all data VI holds about you (article 15(3)). This right can be enforced at any time by contacting VI directly. We endeavour, where plausible, to deliver this information in the most realistic communication transferable within 30 days from submitting your request.

To ensure that we can offer the best service achievable VI encourages you to regularly maintain your data, insofar as updating any changes or anomalies to prevent inaccuracies. As with the request of access this can be completed by direct contact with VI.

Should you require the deletion or removal of any data (part/full), unless engaged in an ongoing contractual obligation or in affect of our statutory rights, we will facilitate the deletion or removal with 30 days of request (Article 17). Upon the necessary action being carried out, a final confirmation will be provided. Article 7(3).

Security precautions

In order to protect you sensitive data VI employs a two stage method in order to prevent unauthorized access or misappropriation.

Digital

In the interest of digital security VI website runs a Secure Socket Layer (SSL). The effect of this ensures that all data exchange through our website is done so in confidence that it is secure end to end. You can ensure that you are accessing the correct website as it will show a lock icon in the address bar.

Physical

Hardcopy of your sensitive data is managed securely throughout by VI. The full information, apart from that which has been disclosed in this charter, will not be disclosed publicly. This is in order to mitigate the risk of any or part thereof from being compromised. If you have employed our services you will receive a copy that is bound by a non-disclosure agreement.

In the unlikely breach of security, VI will present you within 30 days, a report detailing the full investigation and what parts of your data (if any) may have been compromised.

Complaints

If you feel that we have failed to handle your sensitive data in a manner befitting to law and personally, we encourage you to contact VI directly. We will seek to rectify this at the highest level possible. Furthermore if you are dissatisfied we the response or remedial action carried out, you can contact the Information Commissioner’s Office (ICO) https://ico.org.uk/make-a-complaint/ or 0303 123 1113.
